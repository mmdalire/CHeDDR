Crop Health Detection Drone for Rice Fields

It is a remote-controlled drone that monitors and assess healthiness of the rice field inside of the captured image.

Flight abilities

The drone flies in an acceptable height up to (80 meters) to get the captured aerial view of the rice field through live-feed.

Monitors and assess healthiness

The drone measures the healthiness of the rice field through Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI).

The Team

The drone was made by Computer Engineering graduates from FEU Institute of Technology for our thesis course.

Approach

Research stage

The team chose rice fields as the main subject for measurement since rice is mainly imported and is largely consumed in the Philippines. Throughout the rice's growth and photosynthesis, a certain stage is the basis for determining the healthiness of the rice through a remote sensing technology. With the help of reliable sources and some agricultural experts, they were able to confirm that the research can be possible.

Drone assembly

To make sure that the drone flies according to user's input through a remote control, a thorough research must be done to pick the appropriate components for assembling. It should be properly calibrated, accurately perform different actions done by the user, and secure its flight throughout the operation.

Tools used:

  • MAPIR Survey3N Camera (camera)
  • Pixhawk 2.4.6 Flight Controller
  • QGround Control (calibration of drone)
  • Mission Planner (also for calibration)

    • Connectivity between components

    As the drone was assembled together with the camera and a mini-computer, connection between these devices to ground station is needed for transferring of images and to show the live-feed footage real-time to locate the drone while on flight.

    Tools used:

  • Raspberry Pi 3B+(microcomputer used)
  • Python (programming language)
  • RPI.GPIO (library for RPi)

    • NDVI assessment

    Healthiness detection (no detection for diseases and pests in the plant) was made possible by using a remote sensing technology called NDVI. Each pixel of an image is being computed for NDVI value and being categorized according to its healthiness level. The app displays the measured image together with the legends for ease of readability for non-technical users.

    Tools used:

  • C++ (programming language)
  • OpenCV 3.6 (library for image processing

    • GUI application creation

    CHeDDR serves as the main application for users who wants to measure images being transferred from drone, proper naming of the batched images to be processed, and viewing of the measured images in the file system.

    Tools used:

  • Java (programming language)
