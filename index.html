Crop Health Detection Drone for Rice Fields

It is a remote-controlled drone that monitors and assess healthiness of the rice field inside of the captured image.

Flight abilities

The drone flies in an acceptable height up to (80 meters) to get the captured aerial view of the rice field through live-feed.

Monitors and assess healthiness

The drone measures the healthiness of the rice field through Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI).

The Team

The drone was made by Computer Engineering graduates from FEU Institute of Technology for our thesis course.

Prototype

Crop Health Detction Drone for Rice Fields (CHeDDR) is a prototype that aims to monitor and assess the healthiness of the rice fields using multispectral sensor and incorporated in drone technology.

Features

Flight • The drone can be calibrated first to check whether the certain motors and the flight controllers are functional and ready for flight.

• The drone can be controlled by a remote and the user can control its flight for up to six minutes within 20 meters of elevation.

• There is a failsafe mechanism where the drone can safely land during an emergency.

Connectivity • While the drone is in flight, the user can be able to view the live feed on the ground station with the video receiver plugged in.

• After the flight, the drone can automatically send the images to ground station using the drone's hotspot and File Transfer Protocol (FTP). In that way, the user won't have to manually get the SD card of the camera and place on ground station for transfer.

Healthiness • Images are computed and assessed by the NDVI for displaying the partitioned color on which area is healthy and unhealthy together with a legend for user's readability. These are the criterias:

  • Very healthy
  • Moderately healthy
  • Unhealthy
  • Dead plant or inanimate object

CHeDDR app • CHeDDR app serves as the main application for processing the images from the file transfer and accessing the processed images stored in the file system.

Objectives

Healthiness

To display the healthiness of the rice field partitioned by color with its corresponding health condition together with a legend for user's readability.

Flight

To fly the drone with stability and accuracy according to the user's control through a remote controller.

Connectivity

To display live feed from drone's camera and to transfer images from drone to ground station for image processing.

Purposes

Rice is consumed everyday

Here in the Philippines, rice is one of the main imports and the 9th largest consumer of rice. It is already considered as the most substantial food crop that fed a lot of people.

Reducing manual operation

Most farmers today use the conventional method of determining the healthiness of rice through the use of Leaf Color Chart (LCC). The farmers personally compares the greeness of the leaf to a scale which can cost time and energy into going deeply in the farm.

Accuracy of data

Whenever the farmers use LCC as their basis in comparing healthiness, it may have a risk in getting the wrong assessment and inaccuracy of data since it is subjectively graded.

Beneficiaries

Local Government Units

With the gathered data, the LGUs will be able to examine the conditions of rice production and to perform statistical analysis on crop growth and yield to create an effective plan for better quality.

Farmers

This prototype aims to monitor healthiness automatically through drone surveying and Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) assessment instead of conventional methods used by farmers such as Leaf Color Chart (LCC).

Consumers

The consumers would be more assured in purchasing rice from markets since it is under the influence of our prototype.

Approach

Research stage
The team chose rice fields as the main subject for measurement since rice is mainly imported and is largely consumed in the Philippines. Throughout the rice's growth and photosynthesis, a certain stage is the basis for determining the healthiness of the rice through a remote sensing technology. With the help of reliable sources and some agricultural experts, they were able to confirm that the research can be possible.
Drone assembly

To make sure that the drone flies according to user's input through a remote control, a thorough research must be done to pick the appropriate components for assembling. It should be properly calibrated, accurately perform different actions done by the user, and secure its flight throughout the operation.

Tools used:

  • MAPIR Survey3N Camera (camera)
  • Pixhawk 2.4.6 Flight Controller
  • QGround Control (calibration of drone)
  • Mission Planner (also for calibration)

    • Connectivity between components

    As the drone was assembled together with the camera and a mini-computer, connection between these devices to ground station is needed for transferring of images and to show the live-feed footage real-time to locate the drone while on flight.

    Tools used:

  • Raspberry Pi 3B+(microcomputer used)
  • Python (programming language)
  • RPI.GPIO (library for RPi)

    • NDVI assessment

    Healthiness detection (no detection for diseases and pests in the plant) was made possible by using a remote sensing technology called NDVI. Each pixel of an image is being computed for NDVI value and being categorized according to its healthiness level. The app displays the measured image together with the legends for ease of readability for non-technical users.

    Tools used:

  • C++ (programming language)
  • OpenCV 3.6 (library for image processing

    • GUI application creation

    CHeDDR serves as the main application for users who wants to measure images being transferred from drone, proper naming of the batched images to be processed, and viewing of the measured images in the file system.

    Tools used:

  • Java (programming language)
