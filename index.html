Crop Health Detection Drone for Rice Fields

It is a remote-controlled drone that monitors and assess healthiness of rice fields inside of the captured image.

Flight abilities

The drone flies in an acceptable height up to (80 meters) to get the captured aerial view of a rice field through live feed.

Monitors and assess healthiness

The drone measures the healthiness of rice fields through Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI).

The Team

The drone was made by Computer Engineering graduates from FEU Institute of Technology for their thesis course.

Prototype

Crop Health Detction Drone for Rice Fields (CHeDDR) is a prototype that monitors and assess the healthiness of rice fields using multispectral sensor and incorporated in drone technology.

Features

Flight • The drone can be calibrated first to check whether the motors and flight controllers are functional and ready for a flight.

• The drone can be controlled by a remote and the user can control its flight for up to six minutes within 20 meters of elevation.

• The drone has a failsafe mechanism where it can land safely during an emergency.

Connectivity • While the drone is on flight, the user can view the live feed (the video receiver plugged in from the ground station).

• After flight, the drone automatically sends images to ground station using the drone's hotspot and File Transfer Protocol (FTP). In that way, the user won't have to manually get the SD card of the camera and then place on ground station for transfer.

Healthiness • Images were computed and assessed by the NDVI to display the partitioned color on which area is healthy or unhealthy, with a legend for user's readability. These are the criterias:

  • Very healthy
  • Moderately healthy
  • Unhealthy
  • Dead plant or inanimate object

CHeDDR app • CHeDDR app serves as the main application for processing images from the file transfer and accessing these images stored in the file system.

Objectives

Healthiness

To display the healthiness of a rice field through partitioned color based on its corresponding health condition with a legend for user's readability.

Flight

To fly a drone with stability and accuracy according to the user's control using a remote controller.

Connectivity

To display live feed from drone's camera and to transfer images from drone to ground station for image processing.

Purposes

Rice is consumed everyday

Here in the Philippines, rice is a staple which makes us the 6th largest consumer of rice (as of today). It is already considered as the most substantial food crop that fed a lot of people

Reducing manual operation

Most farmers today use the conventional method to determine the healthiness of rice using the Leaf Color Chart (LCC). Farmers compare the greenness of a leaf using a scale which is time consuming and costing energy going deeper into the farm.

Accuracy of data

Whenever the farmers use LCC as their basis for comparison, it may have risk of getting a wrong assessment and data inaccuracy since it is subjectively graded.

Beneficiaries

Local Government Units

The LGUs would be able to examine the conditions of rice production and perform statistical analysis on crop growth and yield to create an effective plan for better quality.

Farmers

CHeDDR automatically monitors healthiness through drone survey and Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) assessment instead of conventional methods used by farmers such as Leaf Color Chart (LCC).

Consumers

Consumers can rest assured when purchasing rice from markets.

Approach

Research stage
The team chose rice fields as the main subject for measurement since rice is mainly imported and is largely consumed in the Philippines. Throughout the rice's growth and photosynthesis, a certain stage is the basis for determining the healthiness of the rice through a remote sensing technology. With the help of reliable sources and some agricultural experts, they were able to confirm that the research can be possible.
Drone assembly

To make sure that the drone flies according to user's input through a remote control, a thorough research must be done to pick the appropriate components for assembling. It should be properly calibrated, accurately perform different actions done by the user, and secure its flight throughout the operation.

Tools used:

  • MAPIR Survey3N Camera (camera)
  • Pixhawk 2.4.6 Flight Controller
  • QGround Control (calibration of drone)
  • Mission Planner (also for calibration)

    • Connectivity between components

    As the drone was assembled together with the camera and a mini-computer, connection between these devices to ground station is needed for transferring of images and to show the live-feed footage real-time to locate the drone while on flight.

    Tools used:

  • Raspberry Pi 3B+(microcomputer used)
  • Python (programming language)
  • RPI.GPIO (library for RPi)

    • NDVI assessment

    Healthiness detection (no detection for diseases and pests in the plant) was made possible by using a remote sensing technology called NDVI. Each pixel of an image is being computed for NDVI value and being categorized according to its healthiness level. The app displays the measured image together with the legends for ease of readability for non-technical users.

    Tools used:

  • C++ (programming language)
  • OpenCV 3.6 (library for image processing

    • GUI application creation

    CHeDDR serves as the main application for users who wants to measure images being transferred from drone, proper naming of the batched images to be processed, and viewing of the measured images in the file system.

    Tools used:

  • Java (programming language)
